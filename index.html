Sir Alex Ferguson

Legendary manager of Manchester United

1941
Sir Alexander Chapman Ferguson is born in Glasgow, Scotland
1957
Debut in Queen's Park's first team as a 16-year-old
1960-1966
Moves to St. Johnstone and then to Dunfermline
1965
Moves to Rangers for a then record fee of 65,000 pounds
1974
Ends playing career at Ayr United
1974
Appointed as East Stirlingshire manager at the age of 32
1974-1978
Manager of St. Mirren
1978
Joins Aberdeen
1983
Wins European Cup Winners' Cup with Aberdeen beating Real Madrid in the final
1986
Named as the new Manchester United manager
1989
Almost gets dismissed as United went on a 7-game winless run
1993
Wins the first league title with Manchester United
1999
Wins the treble (Premier League, FA Cup, UEFA Champions League) with Manchester United
2008
Wins the UEFA Champions League again beating Chelsea in the final with penalties
2013
Retires from management with 13 league titles, 2 European championships, 5 FA cup victories and an overall 49 trophies in his career
2013
After his retirement, he sits on the presidential board of Manchester United
